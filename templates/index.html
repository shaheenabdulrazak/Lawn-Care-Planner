Seasonal Reminders

🌱 Spring (Late April - May)

Action: Apply spring lawn starter (30-0-10 or similar)

Timing: When soil temperature reaches 60°F (usually late April)

Why: Awakens dormant grass and provides nitrogen for new growth

Ontario Tip: Watch for May 2-4 weekend; many start yard work then

☀️ Early Summer (June)

Action: Light feeding if lawn needs it (15-0-15)

Timing: Early June

Why: Supports growth without stressing grass in heat

Ontario Tip: Skip this if lawn is already lush; water instead

🔥 Summer (July - August)

Action: Minimal fertilizer; focus on watering

Timing: Mid-August only if needed (10-0-20)

Why: Cool-season grasses go dormant in heat; fertilizer can stress them

Ontario Tip: Deep watering is more important than fertilizer now

🍂 Fall (September - October) - CRITICAL

Action: Apply fall winterizer (25-0-25 or high-K)

Timing: Late September or early October

Why: Builds root strength and winter hardiness; most important application!

Ontario Tip: This feeding determines how well lawn survives Ontario winters

❄️ Early Winter (November) - Optional

Action: Potassium-only boost (0-0-60) if desired

Timing: Early November before ground freezes

Why: Extra winter survival for excellent lawns

Ontario Tip: Only for well-maintained lawns; skip if average

📋 General Ontario Tips

  • Cool-season grasses peak in spring and fall—fertilize accordingly
  • Avoid fertilizing June-August; it stresses grass in heat
  • Water deeply after each application for best results
  • Check soil pH (6.0-7.0 is ideal) annually
  • Never fertilize frozen ground
  • Local Ontario weather can vary; adjust timing by 1-2 weeks based on your specific area
